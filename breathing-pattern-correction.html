The Pillar Concept

Breathing Pattern Correction

Your breath has a pattern. Patterns can be retrained.

A breathing exercise is something you do for a few minutes at a time — a box breath before a meeting, a few deep breaths before bed. It’s deliberate, conscious, and temporary.

Your breathing pattern is different. It’s how you breathe for the other twenty-three hours and fifty-five minutes of the day — while you work, while you scroll, while you sleep, while you sit in traffic. It’s automatic. Most people have never been asked to pay attention to it.

Breathing Pattern Correction is the process of noticing what that everyday pattern actually looks like, understanding where it came from, and working — deliberately, gradually, and personally — to retrain it.

You may practise a breathing exercise for five minutes. But how are you breathing during the other 23 hours and 55 minutes of your day?

The Difference

Breathing Pattern Correction vs. generic breathwork

Breathwork

A technique or exercise, usually practised for a set number of minutes, often in a group or guided session.

Breathing Pattern Correction

A personalised process focused on the automatic pattern running underneath your entire day — not a single technique.

Format

Delivered exclusively through 1:1 consultations with Sandip, because everyone’s pattern is different.
